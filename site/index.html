Orbin

A modern, fast, and beautiful open-source image board client for Android — built with Kotlin, Jetpack Compose, and Material 3.

Download APK Source on GitHub

Free and MIT-licensed, forever. Not available on Google Play — releases are signed and published on GitHub.

About

Orbin browses vichan/4chan-compatible image boards today, and its provider architecture means new engines (LynxChan, TinyIB, …) can be added by implementing a single interface — no app changes required. It targets Android 15+ and ships regular signed releases.

Subscribed feed

One continuous feed of your followed boards, with in-feed search, tap-to-top chrome, an optional true full-screen mode, and an adaptive tablet layout.

Rich thread viewer

Structured reply trees with quote links and backlinks, inline images and video, collapsible replies, a thumbnail-only grid view, and reading history with scroll restore.

Media done right

Hardware-accelerated playback, pinch-zoom swipe gallery, background preloading, autoplay and mute toggles, and a native download manager with resume and retry.

Gallery & bookmarks

A gallery scoped to your subscriptions with a built-in Bookmarks tab: watch toggles, unread badges, and background thread-watching notifications.

Make it yours

Material 3 with dynamic color, light/dark and AMOLED-black themes, app icon variants, font and thumbnail sizing, and predictive back with smooth transitions.

Private by default

Everything local is encrypted at rest, networking is HTTPS-only with optional DNS-over-HTTPS, and a biometric app-lock keeps the app and its previews gated.

Download

Orbin requires Android 15 or newer. Signed APKs are published on GitHub Releases with SHA-256 checksums for every artifact.

  1. Get the APK from the latest release and allow installs from your browser when Android asks.
  2. Verify the download (optional but encouraged) with the checksum file attached to the release: 
    sha256sum -c orbin-<version>.apk.sha256
  3. Stay up to date. Orbin checks for new versions inside the app (Settings → Network & privacy → Internal updater — on by default), or watch the releases page.

Privacy & security

Privacy in Orbin is structural, not a settings checkbox.

Encrypted at rest

History, bookmarks, downloads, and searches live in a SQLCipher-encrypted database; settings in an encrypted DataStore. Keys are hardware-backed and never leave the TEE/StrongBox.

Locked down

Biometric app-lock gated on a real Keystore cryptographic operation, with FLAG_SECURE keeping locked content out of the recents preview.

Quiet on the wire

HTTPS-only networking enforced end-to-end, optional DNS-over-HTTPS (Cloudflare, OpenDNS, NextDNS), opt-in search history, and no cloud backup of local data.
